Reference


Analog (signal)

An input or output voltage level that can vary in value anywhere between two values. In our case, the Pocket Cloud can sense an analog signal varying from 0-1 Volt.

Digital (signal)

An input or output voltage level that indicates one of two values... basically ON and Off.

Input/Output

An individual pin on a microcontroller may be able to sense voltages being applied to the pin (input) or may be capable of generating its own voltage (an output). Basically the direction of data flow.

Microcontroller

A small computer chip that is used in small control applications such as appliances and other electronic equipment. It is essentially a complete computer which can be programmed to use the other physical capabilities of the chip.

PWM

Stand for Pulse Width Modulation. It is a technique for simulating analog output (variable voltage) on a digital pin by rapidly switching the pin on and off.

Shield

As small add-on board designed to plug into the top of the Pocket Cloud module. It will use certain pins to add functionality to the module.

SSID

This is the name for a WiFi Access Point that is used to connect to.
